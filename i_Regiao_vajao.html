-Region’s history-


Varjão do Torto, referred to by the acronym VJ, instead of a city, is mostly considered a neighborhood or a village, which was inserted in the geographic space of the AR (Administrative Region) of Lago Norte. It is located in the southwest of the Taquari Housing Sector (SHTQ), according to data taken in 2018, the number of inhabitants is estimated at 8,802, and, after some research (PDAD - District Research by Household Sample), the number of urban households reaches 2,499. The city corresponds to around 90.68 ha, between Estrada Parque Paranoá, Ribeirão do Torto and to the West by Public Area.

Its history began in the 1950s, when its lands were still private, belonging to Fazenda Brejo or Torto, and were located in the municipality of Planaltina. Some time later, the first families began to appear, in search of land for agricultural activities, and between the years 1977 and 1982 divisions were made and lots were reciprocated. The big change came in 1991, when the GDF signed Decree nº 13.132, of April 19th, thus creating Vila Varjão, and making permanent the dwellings that already existed there. In the following years, new blocks were built, the Urbanization and Legalization Commission of Vila do Varjão was created, and finally the Administrative Region of Varjão, RA XXIII, by means of Law Nº 3.153, of May 2003. Today, the event completes 19 years old.

Its population is mostly made up of immigrants from the Northeast, and this can be seen through cultural influence - food, language, habits, religiosity, cultural manifestations, etc. For a long time, Varjão was the target of invasions, and this, in a way, generated a “fame” as a violent city.

According to the PDAD, percentage data record that: 84%, 95% of the constructions are permanent, 11.73% are improvised, while 69.26% are houses, 11.50% shacks, 9.29% apartments and 7, 74% kitchenette/studio. While rates in relation to schooling indicate that individuals aged 25 or over (49.3% of the population) claim not to have completed elementary school.

Among several points, the Varjão Recycling Center stands out, from which it recycles around five tons of garbage per day collected from the city and Lago Norte; the Health Center, the Center for Living Together for the Elderly, the events shed, the house of culture, ten sports courts throughout the city, eight squares, five community meeting points (PEC), and the ecological park. .

-Landmarks and their stories-



-Córrego do Urubu (waterfall):

Considered by many to be one of the simplest waterfalls to find, for those who like to hike, it takes an average of 10 minutes to reach it, in addition to being completely free. It is 14 km from the Plano Piloto bus station, in the rural center of Lago Norte , close to the Setor de Mansões lane. It is one of the main springs that supplies Lake Paranoá.

The small waterfall has a sunbathing area. Many trees cover the areas where families gather to rest with their pets. However, this homely atmosphere is lost during weekend afternoons, when several groups with grills and styrofoams with alcoholic beverages arrive at the place. In view of the care for nature, some visitors claim that the preservation of the environment is average, sometimes there are traces of small fires and a little garbage in the leisure area.

In 2009 there was a demonstration by residents of the Núcleo Rural do Córrego Urubu, to alert the population about the preservation of the area. The so-called ‘Save the Urubu’ gained support from the Palha and Torto stream regions, as well as the administration of Lago Norte and Vila Varjão, and the NGO institutions WWF Brasil and Amigos do Futuro. The participation of environmental consultant Andrew Miccolis, who warns about the main threat, which would be the disorderly expansion, and ends by saying that it is important to consider the micro basin as a whole, being an area beyond that of the stream.

  • The "Ecotrails in defense of Águas da Serrinha do Paranoá" project was started in 2018, with the aim of adapting the Pedra dos Amigos trail (Núcleo Rural Córrego do Urubu), for people with visual impairments and wheelchair users;
  • In 2019 the waterfall was banned due to the occurrence of crimes, such as thefts, robberies, persecutions and invasions. For this reason, the 24th Military Police Battalion of the DF began a patrol in the area, led by Commander Major Nafêz.

“We are a trio with a lot of history”, jokes sustainable development doctor, Sol Udry, who also participated in the project along with her other two colleagues, nutritionist Soraya Terra and environmental analyst Raquel Milano, all of whom are local residents. They commented on their first awareness work, in 1996, when they started a movement called Save the Visual of Our Capital. “Our intention was to show not only the residents, but the entire population of the DF that they benefit from the care of the watersheds”, says Dr. Udry.

Finally, collections and cleaning of garbage and planting around the region were carried out, so that over time the results were achieved in the recovery and conservation of the stream and the springs.

-Parque Ecológico e Vivencial (ecological and experiential park) :

It was created by Law nº 1053, of 1996, with the objective of containing the urban growth of the administrative city over sensitive areas of cerrado. It is located on Quadra 10, set A of the city. The park has marshy stretches and is home to the Ribeirão do Torto, which rises in the Brasília National Park and is one of the main watercourses that flows into the northern branch of Lake Paranoá. Like most, this environment was built to protect streams and rivers, expand leisure spaces and reduce damage caused by floods.

Brasília Ambiental is the main manager of the site. Among some places for leisure and physical activity are: the community meeting point, children's park, co-op, skate park, indoor pavement, wooden pergola and multi-sports court.

Its diversity, in relation to vegetation, is very diverse. In 2015, it was estimated that around 40,000 seedlings of Cerrado species were planted in this area – about 6 ha –, being considered gallery forest, there are more than 30 species, among which stands out the copaiba (Copaifera langsdorffii), ingá -mirim (Inga fagifolia), genipap (Genipa americana) and anglerfish (Enterolobium contortisiliquum), in addition to angicos, yellow ipês, mulungus, oitis, paineiras and paus-pigeons.



-Testimonials from residents-



-Oldest resident :

"My name is Camila de Souza Pires, I was born in 1996 and I am 25 years old. I have lived in Varjão since I can remember, born and raised there, but my family came from Ceará. I already worked in many commercial centers, and nowadays I am a manicure of a beauty salon. Never thought of the possibility of living in VJ, especially great part of my family lives here, and I never felt the need of moving away to another city.

As I have always lived in Varjão, I can say that over time it has changed positively in some aspects, such as better housing conditions, asphalting and construction of leisure and sports squares. From my own experience, I think that Varjão is a good place to live, as it has better access to some points, such as housing and food." - Camila Pires, resident of Varjão for 25 years.



-Newest resident :

I wasn’t born in the city, but I moved here with my family very young, I was 2 years old, if I’m not mistaken. Since I was small I played on the courts and enjoyed walking and riding my bike, mainly when I was going to the Córrego do Urubu, which is a heavenly place. Like any city, Varjão has its ups and downs, despite being in Plano Piloto, the city has many negative points, such as basic sanitation and security, which is also questionable depending on the location, and it is often these reasons that lead me thinking about moving away from the city, despite being my point of comfort." - Anonymous, resident of Varjão for 15 years.
